Progesterone is the calm to estrogen's storm. It is the hormone responsible for preparing the body for pregnancy and maintaining it once conception occurs.

## What progesterone does

After ovulation, progesterone levels rise to prepare the uterine lining for a fertilised egg. If pregnancy does not occur, levels drop and menstruation begins.

## Its role beyond reproduction

Progesterone also acts as a negative feedback signal to the brain, telling it to reduce estrogen production. This balance between the two hormones is what keeps your cycle regular and your mood stable.

## When progesterone is low

Low progesterone can cause irregular periods, anxiety, poor sleep, and difficulty maintaining a pregnancy. It is more common than most people realise.
